Research

QSO survey/ slitless spectroscopy/ ML

My research interests focus on the quasar (QSO) selection and properties estimation from slitless spectroscopy. Currently, I'm using the JWST FRESCO/CONGRESS data to understand the QSO near-infrared broad emission lines (like HeI, Pa$\alpha$), and try to give an investigation for further selection and properties estimation by those emission lines.

My Ph.D. thesis centers on the 3D-HST survey and the Chinese Space Station Telescope (CSST) simulation data for similar purposes. Using the Grizli modeling method, 3D-HST G141 grism data could select a more complete sample in redshift 0.8-1.6. On the other hand, our simulation shows that the CSST slitless spectroscopy survey has the potential to discover about 1.3 million new quasars in redshift 0-5 and provide important contributions to AGN science and cosmology.

In broader terms, I have also collaborated on other QSO properties, like searching for changing-look QSOs and investigating QSO variability through X-ray, optical, and infrared photometry and spectroscopy.
